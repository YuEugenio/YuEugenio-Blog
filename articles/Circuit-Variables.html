Circuit Variables: Core Concepts

Aug 17, 2025 · Notes · Circuit Analysis

Core Concepts

(1) Voltage (Potential Difference)

Energy required to move a unit charge through an element, measured in volts (V).

$$v = \frac{\mathrm{d}w}{\mathrm{d}q}$$

(2) Current

Rate of flow of electric charge, measured in amperes (A).

$$i = \frac{\mathrm{d}q}{\mathrm{d}t}$$

(3) Power and Energy

Power $p$ is the rate at which energy $w$ is generated or absorbed over time, measured in watts (W).

$$p = \frac{\mathrm{d}w}{\mathrm{d}t} = \frac{\mathrm{d}w}{\mathrm{d}q}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}q}{\mathrm{d}t} = v\,i$$

(4) Passive Sign Convention (PSC)

A standard convention to determine whether power is absorbed or supplied by an element. Mark one terminal "+" and the other "-".

  • If the reference current enters the terminal marked "+" : Absorbing power : $p > 0$ (element consumes power).
  • If the reference current leaves the terminal marked "+" : Supplying power : $p < 0$ (element delivers power).
