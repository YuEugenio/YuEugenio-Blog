Circuit Elements: Sources, Resistance, and Kirchhoff's Laws

Aug 17, 2025 · Notes · Circuit Elements

Notes for Chapter 2 on circuit elements: ideal sources (independent and dependent), electrical resistance and Ohm's law, and Kirchhoff's laws (KCL/KVL). Equations are formatted with LaTeX and figures are embedded from the course materials.

1. Voltage and Current Sources

An ideal source maintains a prescribed voltage or current across its terminals, regardless of the current/voltage across those terminals.

1.1 Independent Ideal Sources

The source value is specified by itself alone (independent of the rest of the circuit).

1.2 Dependent (Controlled) Ideal Sources

Source value depends on some other voltage or current in the circuit. The four ideal controlled-source types are:

  • Voltage-controlled voltage source (VCVS): $v_s = \mu v_x$
  • Current-controlled voltage source (CCVS): $v_s = \rho i_x$
  • Voltage-controlled current source (VCCS): $i_s = \alpha v_x$
  • Current-controlled current source (CCCS): $i_s = \beta i_x$

These ideal sources are active elements (capable of delivering energy). Passive elements (e.g., resistors) cannot deliver energy.

2. Electrical Resistance (Ohm's Law)

Ohm's law: $v = iR$. The reciprocal of resistance is conductance $G$.

$$G = \frac{1}{R}\;\text{(siemens, S)} = \frac{1}{R}\;\text{(mho, \u2127^{-1})}$$

Power in a resistor (always absorbed for passive sign convention):

$$p = vi = i^2 R = \frac{v^2}{R}$$

3. Kirchhoff's Laws

3.1 Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

The algebraic sum of currents at any node is zero.

3.2 Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

The algebraic sum of voltages around any closed path is zero.
