Simple Resistive Circuits

Date: 2025-08-17

1. Resistors in Series

Equivalent:

$$ R_{eq}=\sum_{i=1}^{k} R_i \;=\; R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_k $$

2. Resistors in Parallel

$$ \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \frac{1}{R_i} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{R_k} $$
$$ G_{eq} = \sum G_i $$

3. Voltage and Current Divider

Voltage divider

$$ v_1 = v_s \\frac{R_1}{R_1 + R_2} \\ v_2 = v_s \\frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2} $$

Current divider

$$ i_1 = i_s \\frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2} \\ i_2 = i_s \\frac{R_1}{R_1 + R_2} $$

4. Voltage and Current Division Equations

Voltage Division Equation

$$ v_j = \frac{R_j}{R_{eq}} \, v \\ R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} R_i $$

Current Division Equation

$$ i_j = \frac{R_{eq}}{R_j} \, i \\ R_{eq} = R_1 \\parallel R_2 \\parallel \cdots \\parallel R_n = \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{R_i} \right)^{-1} $$

6. Measuring Resistance - The Wheatstone Bridge

We use this structure to determine the unknown resistor Rx, using 2 known resistors R1, R2, and a variable resistor R3, if the meter is 0 after adjusting R3, that means

$$ \frac{R_1}{R_3} = \frac{R_2}{R_x} $$

thus

$$ R_x = \frac{R_2}{R_1} \, R_3 $$

7. Delta-to-Wye (pi - to - Tee) Equivalent Circuits

Δ - Y are equivalent, no matter it's Δ or Y, the resistance between 2 nodes must be equal

Thus we can derive:

$$ R_{ab} = \frac{R_c\,(R_a + R_b)}{R_a + R_b + R_c} = R_1 + R_2 \\ R_{bc} = \frac{R_a\,(R_b + R_c)}{R_a + R_b + R_c} = R_2 + R_3 \\ R_{ca} = \frac{R_b\,(R_c + R_a)}{R_a + R_b + R_c} = R_1 + R_3 $$
$$ R_1 = \frac{R_b R_c}{R_a + R_b + R_c} \\ R_2 = \frac{R_a R_c}{R_a + R_b + R_c} \\ R_3 = \frac{R_a R_b}{R_a + R_b + R_c} $$
$$ R_a = \frac{R_1 R_2 + R_2 R_3 + R_3 R_1}{R_1} \\ R_b = \frac{R_1 R_2 + R_2 R_3 + R_3 R_1}{R_2} \\ R_c = \frac{R_1 R_2 + R_2 R_3 + R_3 R_1}{R_3} $$
